Aphasia Reading Exercise

Simulating Difficulties with Reading Comprehension

This exercise simulates reading difficulties associated with aphasia. When you're ready, click the START button to begin. You'll have 45 seconds to read a paragraph silently. Afterwards, answer the questions to the best of your ability.

Trees are perennial plants characterized by a single woody stem or trunk, supporting a crown of branches and leaves. The Coast Redwood, native to California, holds the record for the tallest tree, measuring an impressive 379 feet in height. Interestingly, of the ten tallest known trees globally, four are found in the Western United States, while another four are located in Australia. When it comes to girth, the African Baobab in South Africa takes the lead, with a circumference of 52 feet near ground level. Tree heights vary dramatically, with some species maturing at just a few inches tall. Age-wise, the "Methuselah" pine stands out, with a verified age of 4,844 years. Human intervention in tree planting also has a long history, with the oldest known human-planted tree dating back to 288 B.C., making it 2,299 years old at present.

This exercise simulates the challenges in reading comprehension that individuals with aphasia may experience. By limiting the reading time, we create a scenario where information processing and recall become more difficult, similar to the cognitive challenges faced by those with aphasia.
