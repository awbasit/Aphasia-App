Speaking Impairments Communication Tips.

Take your time and enunciate each word. This helps the person with aphasia process the information more easily.

Give the person time to express themselves. Show that you're listening by maintaining eye contact and nodding.

People with aphasia may need more time to process information and formulate responses. Don't rush them.

Supplement your speech with hand gestures, facial expressions, or pictures to help convey your message.

Ask if they need help finding a word, but allow them to communicate as independently as possible.
